BLINK

Out of all the projects I've made in this course so far, this one was my favorite and the one I'm most proud of. There are a few errors in the drawings but I had a lot of fun creating the different frames. I especially like the fact that they eye closes slightly when the pupil is low. I also enjoy the little speck of light I made to move around the eye as it rolled. It's not extremely accurate but I think it's a charming detail. I wish I'd been able to maintain a transparent background for the gif but unfortunately, I had some issues transferring the layers from Illustrator to Photoshop and it looked like it was the only option if I wanted to use the same files I'd already made.

CUT-UP CINEMA

I spent a lot of much time adjusting this gif and it still doesn't feel exactly right. My goal was to make us raise our hands much more than is seen here, but I wasn't having any luck with moving our arms significantly. We're also dancing a little, which wasn't my intention. I tried to make the frames match each other as much as possible outside of our arm movement but I was having trouble with the points -- I would try to adjust one body part back to normal and our arms or hands would become distorted. I'm satisfied with the final results, although there are some glaring factors that I wish were different (such as our hands getting longer with each frame). All the adjustments I'd made also created some funny moments, where we'd do weird movements or just stand unnaturally.
