    *waiting noises* - A Short Film

    I'm extremely proud of this project! I initially felt daunted when I was assigned to create a short narrative scene. I was unable to think of a concept for some time. After some brainstorming, I came up with a concept I thought I could pull off! I knew I wanted to create something with a comedic edge, so it felt like a no-brainer to rely on voiceover to convey some kind of internal monologue. When I decided to set the film at the entrance of my school, I felt the narrative pieces begin to come together. Most of the shots and VO lines were filmed in one take, which I believe adds to the authenticity of the story -- A slightly messy portrayal of a relatable college experience just feels right in retrospect. However, looking back, there are some glaring areas I can improve. Specifically, I know I can refine the VO for a more seamless sound among the other layers, as well as adjust the visuals for more concision. I plan to revise this project, putting more time into the editing, and re-publishing it in the coming months.

    ROB THE BANK - An Interactive Story

    ROB THE BANK is one of my all-time favorite projects. At first, I was intimidated about building a website from scratch to host this game. However, similarly to my experience hosting my own portfolio, I appreciated the freedom that came with creating a story from scratch. Most illustrations used were created by me, which added an extra layer of personality to the game. In the past, I used to avoid projects that challenged me artistically. This project was one of the first times I tried to outdo my own expectations for my art. I'm most proud of the hallway when walking towards the lasers, the bank in the title screen, and the security guards.

    Looking back, there are a number of things I would've done differently in order to ensure a smoother and more visually appealing product. Namely, most of my image maps came out blurry due to excessive format conversions, and I wish I hadn't relied on publicly-sourced visual elements as much as I had. I'm excited by the new perspective I've gained since I created ROB THE BANK because it proves I'm constantly evolving as an artist. I'm proud of the story I told with ROB THE BANK and I look forward to using the knowledge I've gained from it to create bigger, better stories in the future.




    Exploring Thomas Hunter Hall - A Short Film

    With this project, I set out to capture the essence of Thomas Hunter Hall, a building in the Hunter College campus. I found the areas featured by mistake after getting lost during my first week at Hunter. I remembered the building when I was given the assignment of defining a place through film. The staircases and hallways were filled with artwork that I knew would make a great exploration. I wanted to highlight the pottery hallway in particular – many student projects are taken off display once they’re graded, so I set out to create a new life for the pottery. I filmed from the perspective of a lost student, with each floor more confusing than the last. I welcomed the unstable movements and dim moments that I experienced walking and up the stairs weeks before. I appreciated the opportunity to closely examine the art I would've otherwise overlooked. Hunter's students have an incredible amount of talent to display and I'm glad I got to encapsulate it in a format I can keep with me after I graduate. I would never have seen Thomas Hunter Hall had I not gotten lost that day, and I hope this film can encourage people to explore parts of their life they wouldn't normally think twice about.


    Audio Portrait - Derick

    This project showcases an in-depth interview I conducted with a classmate of mine, Derick. My goal was to create a comprehensive image of Derick through his story retelling. My questions focused on his experience in college as a film major with an interest in the Japanese language. They also explored some of his daily struggles – as a student who also works part time and lives alone, Derick had a unique story to tell about chasing his dreams in New York City.

    Overall, I'm proud of how this interview turned out. It was one of my first times editing strictly sound, and I believe this project can be elevated by the addition of music or relevant sound effects. I also see room for improvement by either making it longer to expand on its themes, or making it more concise in its subject matter for more clarity.


    JCHHOMES Interview Video

    This is one of the bigger projects I’ve taken on recently. For the first time, I oversaw the production of this video from conception to publishing. I produced this project for JCHHOMES, a nonprofit organization based in Queens. They work with the goal of providing services to populations at risk of homelessness. Throughout my time interning at JCHHOMES, I collaborated with my coworkers on projects that would allow us to obtain school supplies for students at a Queens elementary school. Our goal was to collect in-kind donations, as well as raise at least $1000 through social media fundraising. I created this video, an interview with a parent coordinator at the elementary school, in order to raise awareness about the struggles of at-risk children and the initiatives JCHHOMES has taken to make a difference. I prepared a number of interview questions for the parent coordinator with the aim of engaging audiences interested in social issues and making a difference, and encouraging them to explore giving back through JCHHOMES.
